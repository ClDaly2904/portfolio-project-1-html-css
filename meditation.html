Meditation

What is meditation?

Meditation is a practice in which an individual uses a technique focusing the mind on a particular object, thought, or activity to train attention and awareness, and achieve a mentally clear and emotionally calm and stable state.
Meditation uses a combination of positions and breathing techniques to unite body and mind.

Why practice meditation?

  • Gaining a new perspective on stressful situations.
  • Building skills to manage your stress.
  • Increasing self-awareness.
  • Focusing on the present.
  • Reducing negative emotions.

How do I meditate?

Meditation can be simpler than most people think, yet takes a lot of practice to truly find your peace of mind.
Make sure you find yourself a quiet place to relax then use the following steps, video and pose example to guide you as you begin your journey to self-improvement.

">
  1. Take a seat.
    Either follow the pose demonstrated to you with legs crossed, or find what feels good and grounding for you.
  2. Set a timer.
    Starting with small chunks of five or ten minutes and building up to longer periods of time can help you focus when just starting out.
  3. Pay attention to your body, feel your breath.
    Focus on the way that your breathes flows through your body.
  4. Notice when your mind wanders
    Your mind will inevitably wander onto other things. When this happens, just acknowledge it and draw focus back to your breath and sensations in your body to bring you back to the present.
