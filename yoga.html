Yoga

What is yoga?

Yoga orginated in ancient India. It uses physical, mental and spiritual practices, aiming to use the connection between the body and mind to elevate the individual.
Unlike meditation, yoga requires a lot of movement and is also good exercise, but can benefit from the positive headspace found in meditation. There are many poses in yoga to help get the body moving and tap into the enery flowing through it.

Why practice yoga?

  • Improves strength, balance and flexibility.
  • Improves heart health.
  • Helps relax you so you can sleep better.
  • Can increase energy levels.
  • Helps manage stress.

How do I practice yoga?

As a beginner, there are a few basic yoga poses that are great starting points. Watch the video below for your introduction to classics such as Warrior and Mountain Pose, or check out the steps and image below for Downward-Facing-Dog.

  1. Take your place.
    Start off on your yoga mat, and take a moment to relax.

  2. Get onto all fours.
    Slowly swing your legs round and make your way onto all fours with your knees slightly bent and hands shoulder-width apart.

  3. Exhale and straighten your legs as much as you can.
    Use your hands to press into the yoga mat whilst you lift your knees off the floor.

  4. Notice how your body feels.
    Breathe into the pose and send that breath to where your body is stretching to the back of the legs before lowering yourself back onto the mat.
